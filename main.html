Why Should You Be Informed About Climate Change?

Climate change is a significant global issue that affects both the living and nonliving. Climate change can be witness through extreme weather events, abnormal high temperatures, significant temperature inconsistencies, rising sea levels, and changes in ecosystems. Climate change leads to environmental degradation, food and water insecurity, and socio-economic instability. Being informed about climate change can help us understand how our own actions contribute to it and how we can make changes in our own lives to reduce our carbon footprint and be part of the solution.

  • Climate change causes significant changes to our plant
    Some examples are: rising sea levels, more frequent and severe natural disasters, and changes in weather patterns
  • Climate change affects the economy
    It affects industries such as agriculture, forestry, and fishing, as well as leading to increased healthcare costs, property damage, and infrastructure costs. Understanding these impacts can help us make better decisions about how we allocate resources and invest in our communities.
  • Human rights are intimately linked with climate change
    Climate change is affecting communities all over the world, including through displacement, migration, and conflict. Being informed about these impacts can help us understand the social implications of climate change and how we can work to create more just and equitable societies.
  • Climate change is REAL!
    As the impacts of climate change become more apparent, policymakers are taking action to address it. Being informed about these policies and their potential impacts can help us understand how we can participate in the decision-making process and advocate for the policies that we believe will be most effective in addressing climate change.

Things YOU can do to mitigate Climate Change

Defeat the Leak

Household leaks not only sink your monthly budget, but it is also a terrible way to drain energy and resources. You can reduce unneccesary resource wastse by sealing doors, windows, and fireplaces to keep warm air in and cold air out. You can also turn off your leaky faucets to make sure water isn't running when pipes are not in use.

Switch to LEDs

LED Bulbs lasts 25% longer than incandescent bulbs and can save you up to 75% on your electric bill. Make the switch – it’s better for our planet and better for your wallet!

Ride a Bike

Biking is not only fun, it also gets you moving, and cuts down on greenhouse gases!

Reuse Recycle Reduce

single-use plastics are done! Grab your reusables and bring it whenever and wherever you go. Upcycle and reuse your plastic and glass containers!
